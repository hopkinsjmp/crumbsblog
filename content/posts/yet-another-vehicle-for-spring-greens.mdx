ingredients: |-
  * Puff pastry (should come as one sheet, rolled)
  * 1 tub ricotta (usually 250g)
  * 4-6 tbsp Greek (or plain) yoghurt
  * 2-3 leeks
  * 150g frozen peas
  * 50g pine nuts
  * Handful mint leaves
  * A few glugs olive oil
  * Salt and pepper, to taste
method: |-
  1. Preheat the oven to 200°C (180°C fan).
  2. Put a little olive oil in a frying pan and set on a medium heat. Slice the leeks into approx. 2cm chunks and throw into the pan. Gently sauté until tender and starting to unravel, but not yet properly browned, approx. 10 minutes.
  3. While waiting for the leeks to cook, unroll the puff pastry onto a baking tray, keeping it on its baking parchment. 
  4. Whip together the ricotta and 4-6 tbsp of Greek (or plain) yoghurt until you achieve a spreadable consistency. Season with salt and pepper and then spoon onto the pastry and spread all over, taking care to leave a 1-2cm gap around the edge.
  5. Once the leeks are done, evenly distribute onto the ricotta-covered puff pastry. Now scatter over the frozen peas.
  6. Bake in the oven for 20-25 minutes, until the centre has swelled and the pastry is golden.
  7. In the meantime, gently toast the pine nuts in a dry pan, keeping an eye so they don’t burn. Roughly chop up the mint leaves. 
  8. Once the puff pastry is ready, take it out of the oven and give it a moment or two to settle. Then sprinkle over the pine nuts and mint.
  9. Now cut into sixths and grab a slice—reserving a second and third for your main and dessert.
storage: Will keep, covered, in the fridge for 2-3 days but best eaten on the day when the pastry is crisp.

I’m so sorry. I just can’t help myself. Following the delightful discovery of how wonderfully sweet little pop-in-your mouth peas pair with the mellow butteriness of sautéed leeks, I’ve been eager to find another vehicle for them so as to justify having more. This time, however, I wanted to add the refreshing and savoury flavour of mint which, to me, is one of the best partners to peas that has ever been stumbled upon.

And it came to me, thanks to my mum. On her recent visit, she brought with her a new recipe book—in my usual preferred style: beautiful, hardback, suitable as a coffee table book and thus slightly pretentious. She’d found it in a cute shop in a little English village called Alfriston, and because of the title and the seller’s claim that the book wasn’t typically available in the UK, she couldn’t help but get it for me.

The book, _Crumbs of Joy_\*—a Scandinavian cookbook produced by Valio, a Finnish dairy company—included a recipe for a puff pastry traybake. Of course, I thought, what could be more simple yet less intrusive than using puff pastry as a platform for all the flavours I wanted to champion, whilst providing all the necessary integrity as a delivery mechanism.

So, I won’t beat around the bush. Here’s my latest pea and leek recipe, perfect for spring (which we supposedly are in right now). I’ve included mint, as mentioned, as well as ricotta and pine nuts to further accent the mellow and sweet flavours while adding tang and crunch. 

\*\*A few notes on the recipe:\*\* 

This is a love dish to myself, so the flavours are all absolutely necessary for me. However, if your self-love also involves crumbling over some feta or goat cheese as a finishing touch (perhaps with a drizzle of honey), adding podded edamame, freckling over lemon zest, or replacing the pine nuts with toasted pumpkin seeds (and thereby expanding the green colour palette)—then what’s it got to do with me?

\*Heikkinen, Oona, et al., _Crumbs of Joy_, (Helsinki: Cozy Publishing, 2025).
